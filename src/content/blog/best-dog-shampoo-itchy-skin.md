## Understanding Why Your Dog Is Itchy

Before choosing a shampoo, it helps to understand what's causing the itch. The treatment for allergy-related itching is different from infection-related itching.

### Common Causes of Itchy Skin in Dogs

**Environmental allergies** are the most common culprit. Pollen, dust mites, and mould can trigger itching that's worst in spring and autumn. You'll often see itching focused on the paws, belly, and ears. Oatmeal-based shampoos (Earthbath, Burt's Bees) provide the best relief here.

**Bacterial or yeast infections** cause red, smelly, flaky skin. These often develop secondary to allergies — the scratching breaks the skin barrier, letting bacteria in. Medicated shampoos (Veterinary Formula, OxyMed) with antifungal/antibacterial ingredients are what you need.

**Dry skin** from low humidity, over-bathing, or poor diet causes generalised itching without redness or smell. Moisturising shampoos with oatmeal and aloe work well, and you may want to add an omega-3 supplement to your dog's diet.

**Food allergies** cause year-round itching that doesn't respond well to topical treatment alone. If your dog itches constantly regardless of season, talk to your vet about an elimination diet.

### When to See a Vet

Shampoo is a support tool, not a replacement for veterinary care. See your vet if your dog's itching is severe or constant, the skin is broken or bleeding, there's hair loss or a bad smell, over-the-counter shampoos aren't helping after 2 weeks, or your dog seems to be in pain.

## How to Bathe an Itchy Dog

Getting the technique right matters as much as the product. Here are some tips:

**Don't bathe too often.** Once a week is the maximum for most medicated shampoos. Over-bathing strips natural oils and can make itching worse. For maintenance shampoos, every 2-4 weeks is usually sufficient.

**Use lukewarm water.** Hot water feels good to us but can inflame already irritated skin. Keep the water comfortably warm, not hot.

**Let the shampoo sit.** Most medicated shampoos need 5-10 minutes of contact time to work. Lather up, then wait before rinsing. This is the step most people skip, and it makes a huge difference.

**Rinse thoroughly.** Shampoo residue left on the skin can cause more itching. Rinse until the water runs completely clear, then rinse a bit more.

**Pat dry, don't rub.** Rubbing with a towel can irritate sensitive skin. Pat gently or use a low-heat blow dryer if your dog tolerates it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use human shampoo on my dog?**
No. Human skin has a pH of around 5.5, while dog skin is closer to 7. Human shampoos are too acidic for dogs and can strip their skin's natural barrier, making itching worse.

**How often should I bathe my itchy dog?**
For medicated shampoos, follow the product instructions — usually 2-3 times per week initially, then once a week for maintenance. For gentle oatmeal shampoos, every 1-2 weeks is typical.

**Can puppies use medicated shampoo?**
Most medicated shampoos are safe for puppies over 12 weeks, but always check the label. For younger puppies, stick with gentle options like Earthbath or Burt's Bees.

**Should I use a conditioner too?**
A moisturising conditioner can help, especially for dogs with dry skin. Look for one with oatmeal, aloe, or coconut oil. Apply after the medicated shampoo has had its contact time.
